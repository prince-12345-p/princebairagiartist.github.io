Biography

Prince Bairagi is recognized internationally for his profound musicality and commanding technique. Born in Mumbai, India, he began piano studies at age five and gave his first public performance at seven. His exceptional talent led to early recognition and opportunities to study with world-renowned pedagogues.

After completing his studies at the Juilliard School, Prince emerged as one of the most compelling pianists of his generation. His performances combine intellectual depth with visceral excitement, revealing new dimensions in standard repertoire while championing contemporary composers.

Beyond concertizing, Prince is dedicated to music education and serves as artistic director of the Bairagi Foundation for Young Musicians, which provides opportunities for underprivileged talent worldwide.

Artist Statement

"Music is the voice of the soul across time and space. When I play, I seek not just to perform, but to communicate the deepest human emotions — joy, sorrow, hope — that transcend language and culture."

— Prince Bairagi, from an interview with The New York Times

Musical Journey

Early Studies

1995-2005

Began piano studies at age 5 with renowned pedagogue Dr. Mehta in Mumbai. Won first national competition at age 9. Recognized as prodigy by age 12.

International Debut

2006

First international performance at Vienna's Musikverein at age 13. Featured on Eurovision "Young Musicians" program.

Juilliard Years

2010-2016

Accepted to Juilliard's Pre-College division at 15. Studied with Maestro Richard Kinley, earning Bachelor's and Master's degrees with honors.

Professional Breakthrough

2017

Van Cliburn International Competition Gold Medalist. Signed with Columbia Artists Management.

Recent Years

2018-Present

Worldwide concert career with performances at major halls including Carnegie Hall, Royal Albert Hall, and Berlin Philharmonie. Recordings for Deutsche Grammophon.
